About subti

Philosophy: Against the slop

In some ways, current AI models are not really what its creators would like them to be. AGI is still pretty far away. A better description of current models is that they are huge statistical prediction machines, dependent on human-provided data and prompts. Not only does this mean that they interpolate rather than extrapolate any "intelligence", it also means that they lack creativity beyond their data. By definition, they will produce a result based on the statistical mean in their data (and algortithmic programming) for whatever the user is asking for.

I think it is a misconception -- or maybe it is just a confusion of terms -- the idea or wishful thinking that LLM's are really AGI, that has led people to focus on LLM's as chattable, antropomorphic "agents". But it is probably more correct to see large language models as a kind of interactive, non-static statistical dictionaries.

Even if we stop seeing AI agents as people, we can harvest enormous benefits from them. By removing the chat component of AI prompts, subti is an attempt to more effectively make use of the language strengths of LLM's. Of course, all of the functions of subti can be performed manually through a chat. But it will be slower.

Large language models are revolutionizing the way we interact with text. By predicting the next word in a particular phrase, they are incredibly good at producing eerily natural-sounding text. This is not only positive. For instance, the blandness and statistical meanness they produce is probably ill-suited for composing novels or writing essays. In the long run, it is possible that they will force universities and schools to go back to hand-written assignments.

Making use of LLM's does not have to be lazy or sloppy. They do not have to make us dumber. They can be brilliant tools for many of the tasks involved in writing and developing our own skills. For instance, they offer a type of mirror against which we can compare writings with the statistical mean. This is particularly useful for learning a new language. The "mean" of that language is exactly what the learner needs to connect with in order to become really fluent.

If we look at LLM's as tools, they are the best synonym finders and translation tools available. Subti makes it easy to harvest this capability, without the need to chat.

About the creator

My name is Björn. As a polyglot freelance translator and developer, I have often been frustrated with the slowness of the old tools for language learning and translation: dictionaries, synonym dictionaries, translators, etc. The idea to subtitle written text on the computer came to me long before the current LLM boom. As a Swede, I had often contemplated the positive societal effects that subtitles can have on language acquisition.

Subti started from that idea. Once the subtitle function was working, I realized that there are many more potential usages of LLM automation in writing. While many apps and even operating systems offer LLM help within themselves, they tend to do so via the use of a chat, or fix this usage to a specific model. Subti is designed to be more dynamic and user-focused.
